> 📚 **Indie App Playbook · Series overview**

A 7-part hands-on series covering the full path of building an indie iOS app for global markets: validate pain, pick a niche, ship MVP, App Store + ASO, cold start, convert + retain, and build a growth loop.

**60 minutes per part**, each ending with a concrete output. Not theory — checklists you can do today.

## Why these 7 parts

The most expensive mistake new indie devs make is **building for three months and then finding out nobody wants it**.

This series is deliberately ordered by "risk first": validation comes first because it's where most apps die. Growth comes last because it doesn't matter if the first 5 steps are broken. Stop at any step that doesn't pass — it's far cheaper than fixing it after launch.

## 7-part roadmap

### 1️⃣ Validate Your App Idea in 1 Hour

**Core question**: Will anyone pay to solve this pain?

Dig 30 real complaints, capture 10 user quotes, ship a fake paywall funnel, drive small traffic, check 3 conversion rates. **Don't build until this passes.**

### 2️⃣ Pick Your App Niche in 1 Hour

**Core question**: Who do you serve — and who do you say no to?

Write your positioning as one 15-second sentence: "for [who], in [situation], get [result]". Cut every feature that isn't the main reason to pay.

**Core question**: Can a stranger complete the core action in 30 seconds?

Minimal stack: SwiftUI + RevenueCat + PostHog. Core action only, 4–5 step onboarding, basic paywall, key events instrumented.

## How to use this series

**First indie app**: Do them in order. Don't skip ahead. Finish one before opening the next.

**Already in motion**: Start from where you're stuck (most people are stuck on 04 App Store or 05 cold start).

**Live but not earning**: Jump to 06 conversion + retention — most likely your first 90 seconds are broken.

## What this series doesn't teach

- ❌ Swift syntax (go to [Swift's official tutorial](https://www.swift.org/getting-started/))
- ❌ General product methodology (read *Lean Startup* and *Hooked*)
- ❌ Fundraising (indie devs don't need it)

Only this: **how an indie dev takes an idea from zero to $1k/month** — what to do at each step, what to measure, and when to quit.
